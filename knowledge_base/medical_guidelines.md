# Medical Guidelines for Body Scan Metrics

## Vital Signs
- **Heart Rate**: Normal resting heart rate for adults ranges from 60 to 100 beats per minute (bpm). Values below 60 bpm indicate bradycardia, while values above 100 bpm indicate tachycardia.
- **Breathing Rate**: Normal respiratory rate for adults is 12 to 20 breaths per minute. Rates below 12 indicate bradypnea; above 20 indicate tachypnea.
- **Heart Rate Variability (HRV)**: A measure of the variation in time between each heartbeat. For most adults, normal HRV ranges between 20 and 50 milliseconds. A higher HRV generally indicates a healthy, flexible autonomic nervous system, while a low HRV (e.g., persistently under 20 ms) may reflect stress, fatigue, or potential cardiovascular issues.

## Body Composition
- **Body Fat Percentage**: Essential fat values are 2-5% for men and 10-13% for women. Athletic levels are 6-13% (men) and 14-20% (women). Fitness levels are 14-17% (men) and 21-24% (women). Acceptable levels are 18-24% (men) and 25-31% (women). Over 25% (men) and 32% (women) classify as obese and carry higher risk for metabolic conditions.
- **Body Proportions**: Ratios such as shoulder-to-hip width can be a proxy for waist-to-hip ratio, which assesses central adiposity. Higher central adiposity correlates directly with higher visceral fat and cardiovascular risks.
